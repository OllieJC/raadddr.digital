What's this?

RAADDDR is a practical loop for digital delivery. It mirrors observe-orient-decide-act: learn from research, orient with analysis, decide through architecture and design, act with development and delivery, and observe again while you run the service.

It suits agile and iterative ways of working. Plan thin slices, release often, and let the results from Run feed the next Research and Analyse steps. Roles overlap so teams stay aligned on value, risk, and readiness.

Use the breakdown below as a checklist for discovery, sprint goals, release readiness, and operating models, keeping operations and feedback visible from day one.

RAADDDR Framework

The complete lifecycle for modern digital products: Research to Run.

RAADDDR keeps teams aligned to outcomes. It connects discovery to delivery and operations so every iteration is deliberate and measurable.

It mirrors the observe-orient-decide-act loop: learn from research, orient with analysis, decide through architecture and design, act with development and delivery, and observe again in Run.

It fits agile and iterative delivery: short sprints, thin slices of value, inspect-and-adapt every loop, and roles collaborating across each stage.

Methodology Breakdown

  • R - Research Gather evidence from users and stakeholders to frame the problem, risks, and measures of success. Roles: User Researcher, Service Designer, Insights Lead
  • A - Analyse Translate findings into insights, hypotheses, and options, shaping value slices, measures, and decision points. Roles: Business Analyst, Data Analyst, Product Owner
  • A - Architect Shape the service and technical approach - data, security, integration, and operational constraints - balancing speed, scale, and resilience. Roles: Technical Architect, Solution Architect, Security Architect
  • D - Design Define journeys, interactions, and content with accessibility and performance in mind, ready for build and test. Roles: Interaction Designer, Content Designer, Accessibility Specialist
  • D - Develop Build working software and infrastructure with automated tests, telemetry, and secure defaults. Roles: Software Engineer, Frontend Developer, QA Engineer
  • D - Deliver Plan releases, manage change and risk, and keep teams unblocked so increments reach users safely and predictably. Roles: Delivery Manager, Product Manager, Scrum Master
  • R - Run Operate the live service with observability, incident response, change control, and clear service levels. Roles: Site Reliability Engineer, DevOps Engineer, Service Manager
